Q9. Where is “value for money” praised vs. criticized?

Why

To inform pricing policy decisions, optimize bundle passes, and identify discount opportunities.

How

  • Perform topic and sentiment analysis focused on terms such as “price”, “expensive”, “cheap”, “worth it”.
  • Compare frequency and polarity of “value” mentions across attractions and time periods.
  • Estimate price-sensitivity or perceived fairness metrics as elasticity proxies.

Outputs

  • Value perception heatmap highlighting satisfaction by pricing sentiment.
  • Attraction-level “value index” leaderboard.
  • Elasticity or satisfaction–price correlation charts.

Q10. Which add-ons (guides, skip-the-line, audio) improve perceived value?

Why

To optimize product packaging, cross-selling, and upselling strategies for attractions and partners.

How

  • Identify reviews mentioning add-on keywords (“guided tour”, “skip the line”, “audio guide”, etc.).
  • Measure rating differentials and sentiment uplift between users who mention add-ons vs. those who don’t.
  • Analyze co-occurrence networks to detect synergistic product bundles.

Outputs

  • Impact charts showing rating uplift by add-on type.
  • Bundle recommendation matrix suggesting profitable combinations.
  • ROI or perceived value improvement estimates (proxy level).
